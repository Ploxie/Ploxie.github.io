Godling

The real-time-strategy game we made at The Game Assembly

Summary

For our sixth game project we got to make a Real-Time-Strategy game.
We wanted to make the game revolve more around the main character than
to order a bunch of units around. So we added special abilities to the main character and have a couple of "minions" that you could controll.

Details

  • Genre: Real-Time-Strategy
  • Development time: 10 Weeks, half time
  • Language: C++
  • 3rd party tools:
    • Unity
    • Wwise
  • Team: 11 Members
    • 6 Programmers
    • 2 Level Designers
    • 3 Artists

Fog of war

One of the things I worked on in this project was the fog of war. It's what determines how much of the
map you can see, as can be seen on the image to the right. The fog of war consists of three parts:
The black area, which is the 'unexplored' terrain.
The darkened area, which is the terrain which is explored, but isn't visible from any units.
The visible area, which is the visible terrain, in this area you can see enemy units.

Instead of generating the fog of war using ray-casting, I decided to send every units world position in a
vertex buffer to the GPU. There I could calculate the 'map-position' and use that and the units vision
range to draw the terrain.

The same had to be done for all the enemies, waypoints, alert-pings, etc.

This also made it easy for us to see which enemies were outside of our vision and we could then disable
them from being drawn.
